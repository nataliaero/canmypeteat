## Why Pineapple Is Good for Dogs

Pineapple is more than just a tasty tropical fruit — it offers several health benefits for your canine companion:

- **Vitamin C** — Supports immune system health and acts as an antioxidant
- **Bromelain** — A natural enzyme that aids digestion and has anti-inflammatory properties
- **Manganese** — Essential for bone health and metabolic function
- **Dietary fiber** — Promotes healthy digestion in appropriate amounts
- **Hydration** — Pineapple is about 86% water, making it a refreshing snack

## How to Safely Serve Pineapple to Your Dog

Follow these guidelines to make pineapple a safe treat:

1. **Remove the skin and core** — The tough outer skin and hard inner core are difficult to digest and can cause choking or intestinal blockage
2. **Cut into small pieces** — Bite-sized chunks appropriate for your dog's size
3. **Start small** — Introduce pineapple gradually to check for any digestive sensitivity
4. **Serve fresh only** — Avoid canned pineapple, which contains added sugars and syrups
5. **Keep it occasional** — Treats should make up no more than 10% of your dog's daily caloric intake

## What to Avoid

While fresh pineapple is safe, there are some important warnings:

- **Canned pineapple** — The syrup contains too much sugar and may include preservatives
- **Pineapple skin and leaves** — These are tough, fibrous, and can cause choking or digestive blockage
- **Pineapple core** — Too hard for dogs to chew and digest safely
- **Large quantities** — Too much pineapple can cause diarrhea or upset stomach due to the high fiber and natural sugar content

## Signs of Trouble

If your dog eats too much pineapple or has a sensitivity, watch for:

- Diarrhea or loose stools
- Vomiting
- Abdominal discomfort
- Loss of appetite

If symptoms persist for more than 24 hours, contact your veterinarian.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can puppies eat pineapple?

Yes, puppies can have small amounts of fresh pineapple once they are fully weaned and eating solid food. Start with very small pieces and monitor for any digestive issues.

### Does pineapple stop dogs from eating poop?

This is a popular home remedy, but there is limited scientific evidence that feeding pineapple prevents coprophagia (poop eating) in dogs. If your dog has this habit, consult your vet for proper behavioral guidance.

### Can dogs eat dried pineapple?

Dried pineapple is safe but has a much higher concentration of sugar per serving. If you offer dried pineapple, give only a very small amount and make sure it has no added sugar or preservatives.
